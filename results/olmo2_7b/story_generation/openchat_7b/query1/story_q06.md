## Lesson Title: The Evolution of Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA)

1. **Introduction (Hook)**: Introduce SOA through an engaging real-world problem or scenario that highlights the need for scalable and maintainable architectures.

2. **Core Content Delivery**: 
   1. The origin of SOA: Evolution from monolithic architectures.
   2. Importance of stateless design in SOA.
   3. Interface abstraction in SOA for standardized communication.
   4. Role of service brokers in enabling service discovery.

3. **Key Activity/Discussion**: Engage students in a group activity or discussion to explore the benefits and challenges of implementing SOA in real-world situations.

4. **Conclusion & Synthesis**: Summarize the lesson by connecting back to the overall summary, emphasizing how SOA's focus on stateless design, interface abstraction, and service brokers contribute to its evolution from monolithic architectures.
