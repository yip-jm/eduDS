```markdown
# Lesson Plan Outline

## Lesson Title:
**Exploring Cloud-Native Design: Microservices, Containers, and Orchestration**

## Introduction (Hook):
- **Objective:** Capture students' interest by presenting a real-world problem, such as how Netflix ensures seamless streaming to millions of users simultaneously using cloud-native technologies.

## Core Content Delivery:
1. **Introduction to Cloud-Native Computing**
   - **Objective:** Define cloud-native computing and explain its significance in modern software development.
   
2. **Microservices Architecture**
   - **Objective:** Describe the principles and benefits of microservices, including modularity, independent deployment, and scalability.

3. **Container Technologies: Docker Overview**
   - **Objective:** Introduce containerization with a focus on Docker, explaining how it encapsulates applications for consistency across environments.
   
4. **Orchestration Tools: Kubernetes Introduction**
   - **Objective:** Explain the role of orchestration tools like Kubernetes in managing containerized applications.

5. **CNCF's Cloud-Native Stack Definition**
   - **Objective:** Outline the CNCF-defined stack and its components, highlighting how they collectively enable cloud-native capabilities.

6. **Case Studies: Netflix and Uber**
   - **Objective:** Analyze examples from companies such as Netflix and Uber to illustrate practical applications of cloud-native design principles.

## Key Activity/Discussion:
- **Objective:** Engage students in a group discussion or activity where they brainstorm potential improvements for an existing application using cloud-native practices.

## Conclusion & Synthesis:
- **Objective:** Summarize the lesson by connecting core concepts back to the overall significance of cloud-native computing, emphasizing its impact on achieving elastic scaling, rapid deployment, and automation.
```

This outline provides a structured approach for educators to deliver content effectively while engaging students with practical examples and discussions.
